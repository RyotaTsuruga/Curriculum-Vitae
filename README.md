## Research Profile

I study the neural mechanisms that govern learning and behavioral adaptation, with a particular focus on dopamine signaling in the tail of the striatum. My research examines how appetitive and aversive information interact to shape behavior.

**Research fields:** Neuroscience, Cognitive Neuroscience, Pharmacology

**Research interests:** Dopamine · Striatum · Appetitive learning · Aversive learning · Sensory stimuli · Fiber photometry · Optogenetics

## Current Research

My current work investigates how dopamine dynamics in the tail of the striatum support adaptation to aversive stimuli and influence appetitive learning. Using behavioral experiments, fiber photometry, and optogenetics, I aim to clarify the neural processes that enable flexible transitions between defensive and reward-seeking behavior.
